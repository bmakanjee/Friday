Standard Operating Procedure
Seat Shampooing & Extraction
Version 1.0  •  March 2026  •  Internal Use Only
1

Equipment Needed

Item Notes
Wet/dry vacuum Ryobi 20L recommended
Spot cleaner / extraction attachment Powerfit or equivalent
Drill with brush attachments Medium bristle for seats, stiff for floor mats
Spray bottles × 2 One product, one clean water
AutoSmart fabric shampoo Dilute 1:10 general • Undiluted for stains
Clean microfibre towels Minimum 4–6 per job
2

Process — Standard Clean

For light soiling. Work one seat at a time, top to bottom.

1
Dry Vacuum 5 min
Full vacuum of all seats, crevices, under headrests, between seats. Get the loose dirt out first.
2
Pre-Spray 2 min
Mist AutoSmart fabric shampoo diluted 1:10. Don't soak. Work section by section.
3
Agitate 5–8 min / seat
Drill brush on medium speed, circular motions, section by section. Spend extra time on high-contact areas (bolsters, headrest).
4
Extract 8–12 min / seat
Spot cleaner attachment on wet/dry vac. Slow overlapping passes, firm pressure against fabric. Minimum 3–4 passes per section.
5
Rinse Extract 5 min
Light mist of clean water only. Extract again. Removes shampoo residue that attracts dirt.
6
Dry 2–3 hrs
Leave windows down / doors open. Minimum 2–3 hours in QLD sun. DO NOT return car with damp seats.
3

Process — Heavy Stains

Coffee, food, pet, kids — the real money jobs.

Heavy Stain Protocol
  • Apply product UNDILUTED directly on the stain
  • Let DWELL 2–3 minutes — chemical needs time to break down stain
  • Agitate with drill brush on LOW speed — contact time > splash
  • Extract, then REPEAT if needed — some stains need 2–3 passes
  • Final pass: rinse extract with clean water
RULE: Never soak the whole seat. Soak the STAIN only. Excess water in foam = mildew risk.
4

Quality Checklist

  • All seats extracted evenly (no missed sections)
  • No visible stains remaining (or documented / communicated to customer)
  • No damp spots — seats dry to touch before handover
  • Floor mats done (front and rear)
  • Boot carpet done
  • Before/after photos taken for socials
5

Pro Tips

  • Work one seat at a time, top to bottom
  • Empty vac tank between seats — suction drops when full
  • Take before/after photos — content gold for socials
  • If heavily soiled, repeat steps 2–4 on problem areas
  • For leather seats: skip shampoo, use leather cleaner + conditioner instead
Total Time Per Car: 45–90 min
6

Common Mistakes

  • Soaking the whole seat — causes mildew
  • Not rinsing after extraction — leaves sticky residue that attracts dirt
  • Rushing extraction passes — need firm, slow passes for proper suction
  • Returning car with damp seats — always check before handover
  • Using stiff drill brush on delicate fabric — test in hidden area first
