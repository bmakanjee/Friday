Process — 2-Stage Correction

Prep
1
Wash
Full decontamination wash. Foam, hand wash, rinse. Vehicle must be spotless before any machine touches it.
2
Clay Bar
Clay entire vehicle with lubricant. Removes bonded contaminants the wash missed. Run your hand over the paint — it should feel like glass.
3
Dry
Full hand dry. Blow out crevices with compressed air or leaf blower. No water spots allowed.
4
Mask
Tape all trim, badges, rubber seals, plastic surrounds. Protects from compound burn. Non-negotiable.
5
Inspect
Use LED light at multiple angles. Identify defects. Mark worst panels. This is your baseline.
Correction
6
Cutting Pass
DA polisher + cutting pad + Menzerna heavy cut compound. Work 60cm × 60cm sections. Medium pressure, slow arm speed, 3–4 passes per section. Wipe residue with microfibre.
7
Inspect
LED light check. Did the defects come out? If not, repeat section. Don't move on until you're satisfied.
8
Finishing Pass
DA polisher + finishing pad + Menzerna finishing polish. Same sections. Light pressure, medium speed. Refines the finish to high gloss.
9
IPA Wipe
Panel wipe all corrected surfaces. Removes polish oils. Reveals true finish — what you see now is the real result.
10
Final Inspect
LED light at all angles. Confirm 80–90% defect removal. Document any remaining deep scratches (may need wet sanding on next visit).
Protection
11
Sealant
Apply spray sealant or ceramic sealant to lock in the correction. All that work means nothing without protection.
12
Final Buff
Light buff with clean microfibre. Remove any sealant residue. Stand back and admire.
1-Stage Shortcut

For 1-stage enhancement: skip steps 6–7 (cutting pass). Go straight to finishing polish with a light cutting pad. Same prep, same protection — less correction.

4

Quality Checklist

  • All panels corrected evenly
  • No holograms (buffer trails) visible
  • No compound residue in panel gaps
  • Trim tape removed cleanly
  • Sealant applied and buffed
  • Before/after photos taken (macro shots showing swirl removal)
  • Paint depth measured before and after (didn't remove too much clear coat)
5

Critical Rules

PAINT DEPTH: ALWAYS check paint depth before cutting. Thin clear coat = less correction possible. Measure, don't guess.
SHADE ONLY: Work in the shade, never in direct sunlight. Compound dries too fast and becomes impossible to remove.
LUBRICATION: Keep pad and surface lubricated at all times. Dry buffing burns paint — no exceptions.
PAD MAINTENANCE: Replace pads when clogged. A saturated pad generates heat, not correction.
MASK EVERYTHING: Tape all plastic and rubber surfaces. Compound stains trim permanently — and it looks amateur.
